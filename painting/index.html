Scheme 01

Heritage Cream & Woodland

The safest option — warm heritage cream walls with deep grey-green trim. Broadly appealing, reads as "well-maintained character home." The existing bullnose iron stays as-is or gets a fresh coat of Woodland Grey.

Why it works: The cream lifts the render out of "90s Tuscan" territory into heritage-neutral. Woodland Grey on trim unifies the bullnose, fascia, and posts into one deliberate frame. Heritage red door adds character without risk. Broadest buyer appeal.
Scheme 02

Sage & Basalt

Why it works: Sage green is having a moment in Australian residential design — it reads as both natural and considered. The dark basalt trim gives the bullnose a modern-heritage crispness. Navy door adds sophistication. Appeals to design-conscious buyers and sits beautifully in the Weston Creek bush setting.
Scheme 03

Warm Stone & Monument

Splits the difference — a warm stone/putty wall that's more sophisticated than cream but less committal than sage. Deep charcoal Monument trim gives a sharp, contemporary edge while respecting the period feel.

Why it works: Warm stone tones are the most timeless option for rendered homes in Canberra — they handle the harsh light well across seasons. Monument is the most popular Colorbond trim colour in Australia right now, so it signals "recently updated." Bottle green door nods to heritage without being predictable. The middle-ground pick.
Next steps: Grab sample pots from Bunnings for your top pick. Paint a 1m² test patch on the front-facing wall — Canberra's harsh western sun will shift these colours significantly between morning and afternoon. Also check the render for any hollow spots (tap test) before committing to a painter. For the bullnose iron, a Colorbond-matched spray in the trim colour will unify everything.
