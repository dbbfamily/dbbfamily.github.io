Key principle: Cut to a fork — never mid-stem, never flat top. New growth comes from fork points. Keep 5–7 of the strongest main stems.
3
Cutting Rules
✓
Dead wood out firstAny completely bare stem with no foliage — cut at base. This alone removes ~40% of the problem.
✓
Cut to a forkFind a natural fork at roughly eye level. Cut just above it. New growth comes from the fork.
✓
Remove crossing centre stemsAnything growing inward through the middle — remove entirely at base.
✓
Step back every 5 cutsAssess from a distance before continuing. Easy to over-cut when you're in close.
✓
Keep 5–7 main stemsThe strongest, most upright ones. Remove the rest at ground level.
✗
Don't flat-top itMeasuring a height and cutting straight across looks terrible and the shape never recovers.
✗
Don't use the hedge trimmerLoppers only. Individual branch cuts, not surface shaping.
✗
Don't remove more than half total volumeEven a neglected bottlebrush can be shocked. Leave enough foliage to photosynthesise.
4
After Pruning
💧
Water well at the baseHelps with the stress of pruning, encourages new root growth.
🌿
Retrofit paper bags around basePull back existing mulch, lay bags, wet, replace mulch. Stops grass competition while it recovers.
📅
Expect new shoots in 3–4 weeksFrom every cut point and fork. By spring it'll be unrecognisable.
